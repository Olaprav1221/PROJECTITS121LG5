OUR STORY


Bentley Motors was established by Walter Owen Bentley, sometimes referred to as "W.O.", in Cricklewood, North London. The company's initial aim was on building high-performance vehicles that combined elegance, dependability, and speed.

Bentley's early success in motorsport, especially in endurance racing, helped it to become well-known. In the 1920s, Bentley automobiles dominated races such as the 24 Hours of Le Mans, which they won five times between 1924 and 1930. These triumphs contributed to Bentley's reputation for dependability and performance.
